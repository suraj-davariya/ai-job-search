# Interview Preparation

---

## STAR Format Definition

The STAR framework structures behavioral interview answers so they are concrete, complete, and easy to follow.

```
S — Situation: Set the scene. What was the context? What was at stake?
T — Task: What was your specific responsibility or challenge?
A — Action: What did YOU specifically do? (Use "I", not "we")
R — Result: What was the outcome? Quantify where possible.
```

**Usage guidance:**

- Keep each component to 1–3 sentences.
- The Result must include at least one concrete outcome (metric, decision, artifact, or behavioral change).
- When quantifying is impossible, describe the qualitative impact clearly and specifically.
- Practice saying each STAR example aloud — if it takes more than 90 seconds, it is too long.

## Tough Questions

Common difficult interview questions with framing guidance.

- **"Tell me about yourself"** — 2 minutes max. Structure: current role summary → relevant background highlight → why this role. This is not a career history recitation.

- **"What's your greatest weakness?"** — Name a real growth area; describe what you have done to address it; show self-awareness. Never use "I work too hard."

- **"Why are you leaving your current role?"** — Frame forward: seeking growth, a new challenge, or a different environment. Never criticize your current employer.

- **"Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"** — Align with the role's growth path. Show ambition without overcommitting to a specific title.

- **"What's your salary expectation?"** — Reference the salary benchmark from `/setup`. If asked early in the process, deflect: "I'd love to understand the full scope first — can you share the budgeted range?"

- **"Tell me about a failure"** — Choose a real failure. Focus 20% on what happened and 80% on what you learned and what you changed. Do not choose a trivial failure.

- **"Why do you want to work here?"** — Must reference a specific, verified fact about the company (product, mission, published research, culture signal). If you cannot find one, do not answer with a generic claim.

## Questions to Ask

Always have at least 3 questions ready. If a question gets answered earlier in the interview, cross it off and ask the next one — never say "you already covered everything."

**Role:**
- What does success look like in the first 90 days?
- What are the most important problems this role is solving right now?

**Team:**
- How does the team typically handle disagreements on technical or product decisions?
- What's the team's working style — more async or synchronous?

**Tech/Process:**
- What does the development/delivery cycle look like end-to-end?
- What's the biggest technical challenge the team is working through right now?

**Culture:**
- How does the company support learning and professional development?
- What do people who thrive here tend to have in common?

## Phone and Video Tips

- Test your audio and camera 10 minutes before the call.
- Use a neutral background or a professional virtual background.
- Look at the camera, not the screen, when speaking.
- Slow down — nervousness speeds up speech; pace yourself deliberately.
- Have your STAR examples and the job posting open but off-camera.
- For phone interviews: stand up — your voice carries more authority when standing.
- Send a brief thank-you email within 24 hours of any interview.

---

## Follow-Up Etiquette

- **Thank-you email within 24 hours:** reference one specific topic from the interview; express continued interest; keep it under 150 words.
- **If you haven't heard back** after the stated timeline: send one follow-up email only, asking for a status update. Do not send multiple follow-ups.
- **If rejected:** thank them for their time; ask for feedback (many interviewers will share it); keep the door open for future opportunities.

## Roleplay Guidelines

How to run a practice session with the AI:

1. Tell the AI: "Run a mock interview for [Role] at [Company]. Start with 'tell me about yourself'."
2. Answer each question as you would in the real interview — do not pause to think aloud.
3. After each answer, the AI will: flag any backtrack-test failures, suggest improvements, and note missing STAR structure.
4. After 5–7 questions, ask the AI for an overall assessment covering fluency, specificity, and question coverage.
5. Repeat the session until you can answer all questions fluently without referencing notes.
