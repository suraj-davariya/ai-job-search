The same terms, in the same meanings, as the project's canonical
[engineering glossary](https://github.com/suraj-davariya/ai-job-search/blob/main/docs/glossary.md)
— phrased here for humans first. Where a term has its own guide page, it's
linked.

## A

**Application Pipeline** — the end-to-end journey [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply)
runs: posting in → fit evaluation, tailored CV, cover letter, review,
compiled PDFs out.

**Application Tracker** — [the CSV file](/docs/your-data) recording every
application: company, role, status, fit rating, and links to the exact
documents sent. Feeds the dashboard and the deduplication in `/search`.

**Application Status Enum** — the seven allowed values of the status column:
`Draft, Sent, Interview, Offer, Rejected, Withdrawn, Closed`. The dashboard
[enforces which moves between them are legal](/docs/dashboard/applications-table#the-status-state-machine).

**Atomic CSV Write** — how the dashboard saves your tracker without ever
leaving a half-written file, even if something interrupts it mid-save: write a
temporary copy first, keep a backup, then swap it into place in one step.

**ATS-Safe Export** — the machine-readable companions [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply#exports-a-robot-can-read-too)
produces beside the polished PDF — a plain-text `.txt` and a Word `.docx` from
the same content — so an applicant-tracking system can parse your CV reliably.
A self-check confirms the text is recoverable from the PDF.

**Allowed-Tools** — an internal safety list declaring which tools each
CareerForge skill may use; anything not listed is refused.

## B

**Behavioral Profile** — the part of your profile describing how you work:
style, strengths, growth areas, preferred environment. Shapes the cover
letter's voice and the culture-fit score.

**Business Rules Engine** — the fixed scoring frameworks and validation rules
behind fit evaluation, content cutting, and the final quality checks.

## C

**Candidate Profile** — the structured record of your identity, education,
experience, and skills. [Built by `/setup`](/docs/commands/setup); the source
of truth every CV claim must trace to.

**Competency Expansion** — discovering skills your documents imply but your
profile doesn't list yet (planned: the `/expand` command).

**Compile-and-Inspect Loop** — after drafting, documents are compiled to PDF
and visually checked — page counts, orphaned headings, font mismatches — and
re-fixed until clean.

**Content Cutting (Relevance-Weighted)** — when a CV runs over two pages, the
lines least relevant to *this* posting are cut first, wherever they sit.

**Cross-Reference Check** — during onboarding, your sources are compared
against each other (CV vs LinkedIn vs diplomas); any disagreement is shown to
you to resolve, never silently picked.

## D

**Deal-Breaker** — a hard constraint of yours (no relocation, minimum salary)
that disqualifies a job regardless of every other score.

**Drafter Agent** — the primary AI in the pipeline: evaluates fit, writes the
documents, applies the reviewer's feedback.

**Document Folder** — `documents/`, where you drop CVs, LinkedIn exports,
diplomas, and reference letters for [`/setup`](/docs/commands/setup) to scan.

## E

**Evaluation Framework** — the five-dimension scoring system:
Technical Skills 30%, Career Alignment 30%, Experience 25%, Behavioral Fit
15%, Location pass/fail. See [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply).

## F

**Fabrication Audit** — the check, run by [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply#every-claim-traced-to-its-source),
that traces every CV claim back to the profile file backing it and flags any it
can't. You read the result in the [Provenance panel](/docs/dashboard/provenance).

**Fit Assessment (Quick)** — the fast High / Medium / Low ranking
[`/search`](/docs/commands/search) gives each new posting. Triage, not the
full evaluation.

**Fit Rating** — the 0–100 weighted score from the full evaluation. Column 9
of [your tracker](/docs/your-data); the fuel for the
[fit charts](/docs/dashboard/charts#fit-distribution).

**Forward-Looking Framing** — the cover-letter principle: write about the
problems you'll solve for them, not a recap of your CV.

## G

**Gap Heatmap** — a prioritized table of skill gaps between your profile and
target postings (planned: the `/upskill` command).

**Ghost Job** — a posting that's advertised but isn't really being filled —
left open to collect résumés, gauge a market, or look like growth. One of the
red flags the [posting legitimacy](#p) check watches for.

## I

**Idempotent Operation** — safe to run twice: re-running `/setup` with the
same inputs duplicates nothing and overwrites nothing.

**Interview Backtrack Test** — the quality bar for every claim: could you
back this sentence in an interview without "well, what I actually meant
was…"? If not, it gets softened or cut.

## J

**Job Portal Adapter** — a pluggable connector for one job board, so new
boards can be added without touching the core (planned for a future
milestone).

## L

**Language Registry** — the set of UI languages CareerForge ships, currently
12 (machine-translated, in beta), with English as canonical. See
[Languages & Locales](/docs/languages).

**Learning Plan** — a structured study guide produced from skill-gap analysis
(planned: `/upskill`).

**Legitimacy Verdict** — the standalone judgment [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply#first-it-checks-the-posting-is-real)
gives a posting — **Verified**, **Caution**, or **Suspicious** — with cited
reasons, kept separate from the fit score so a warning can't be averaged away.

**Locale Pack** — a per-market bundle of CV conventions (document term, page
size and count, photo norm, personal fields, date format, GDPR clauses) that
[`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply) applies for the market you're targeting.
Separate from UI language. See [Languages & Locales](/docs/languages).

## N

**Named Sub-Agent** — an internal mechanism for defining extra AI agents (the
reviewer is one) with their own instructions and, optionally, their own model.

## O

**Onboarding** — [`/setup`](/docs/commands/setup)'s three convergent paths —
scan documents, import a CV, or interview — all ending in the same profile.

## P

**Profile Files** — the seven documents defining your professional identity:
candidate profile, behavioral profile, writing style, evaluation framework,
CV templates, cover-letter templates, interview prep.

**Pipeline KPI** — the dashboard's headline numbers: status counts, average
fit (30 days), interview rate (90 days). See [the KPIs](/docs/dashboard/kpis).

**Posting Legitimacy** — the question of whether a job posting is genuine and
safe to engage with, assessed by [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply#first-it-checks-the-posting-is-real)
before drafting and surfaced as a [Legitimacy Verdict](#l). The system warns;
you decide.

**Profile Statement** — the 3–5 line pitch at the top of your CV, kept in
several variants and tailored per application.

**Provenance Panel** — the dashboard view, in an application's detail drawer,
that shows each CV claim beside the profile source backing it, with unbacked
claims flagged. Read-only. See [the Provenance panel](/docs/dashboard/provenance).

## R

**Read-Only Mode** — a dashboard flag that disables all editing — handy for
demos, screenshots, or guaranteeing an accident-free look around.

**Red Flag** — a warning sign in a job posting — upfront fees, personal-data
harvesting, a mismatched domain, a too-good salary — that feeds the
[Legitimacy Verdict](#l).

**Reviewer Agent** — the second AI in [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply):
fresh perspective, researches the company, critiques both drafts. It
suggests; the drafter (and you) decide.

## S

**Salary Benchmark** — optional salary comparison against data you supply —
union statistics, surveys, your own research (the `salary_lookup` tool).

**Search Queries** — your configured search phrases, organized by priority,
that [`/search`](/docs/commands/search) runs across your chosen portals.

**Seen Jobs Registry** — the local file remembering every posting you've ever
been shown, so `/search` never repeats itself.

**SKILL.md Orchestrator** — the internal anchor file each CareerForge skill
ships with, declaring what it does and which tools it may use.

**STAR Format** — Situation, Task, Action, Result: the interview-answer
structure. `/setup` builds STAR stories from your real experience and leaves
clearly marked stubs where only your memory can fill the details.

## T

**Tracking Dashboard** — [the local web UI](/docs/dashboard) over your
tracker file. Runs only on your machine (`127.0.0.1`), edits only status and
notes, and renders everything else read-only.

**Two-Plane Skill Architecture** — the internal split between knowledge-style
skills (instructions the AI reads) and tool-style skills (external programs
it runs).

**Traceability** — the project's practice of linking every requirement to the
design, code, and tests that satisfy it, via IDs like `REQ-…` and `TC-…`.

## V

**Verification Checklist** — the final pass/fail list `/apply` runs once at
the end: factual accuracy, targeting, consistency, LaTeX quality, and PDF
layout.

## W

**Workspace Settings** — the local configuration file declaring which tools
the AI may use in your copy of CareerForge. Personal, and never committed.
