`/upskill` is your career coach. It looks at the distance between where you are and
where the jobs you want need you to be, then hands you a prioritised list of what to
learn and where to learn it — every resource pulled from a live web search, never
invented.

## Two ways to run it

| You type | Mode | What it looks at |
|----------|------|------------------|
| `/upskill` | **Aggregate** | Every job in your tracker. Roles you fit *least* count most, so the plan points at what's actually holding you back. |
| `/upskill <job url>` | **Targeted** | One specific posting. (Paste the text instead if the link won't load.) |

## What a run looks like

```text
you:    /upskill
claude: Reading your tracker (12 applications) and your profile…

        Skill Gap Heatmap
        ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
        Priority  Skill / Area          Type        Seen in
        Critical  Kubernetes            tooling     7 of 12 roles
        High      Terraform / IaC       tooling     5 of 12 roles
        High      MLOps pipelines       domain      4 of 12 roles
        Medium    Stakeholder comms     soft        3 of 12 roles
        Low       Go                    —           1 of 12 roles
        ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

        Building your learning plan (searching for current resources)…
        Saved → upskill/report-2026-06-17.md
```

The heatmap prints first so you can see the shape of the gap before the detail.

## What you walk away with

1. **A gap heatmap** — every gap rated Critical / High / Medium / Low, tagged by kind
   (a missing tool, a domain you haven't worked in, a soft skill, or a credential).
2. **A learning plan** — for each gap that matters, two or three real resources (with
   links and a one-line reason), a study direction tuned to what you *already* know so
   you don't re-learn the basics, and a time estimate.
3. **A study order** — a numbered sequence that respects dependencies, front-loads quick
   wins, and saves broad domain knowledge for last, with a total time at the bottom.
4. **A saved report** at `upskill/report-*.md`. Run it again next month and the aggregate
   report opens with a *delta*: which gaps you've closed, and which are new.

## How it stays honest

- **No invented resources.** Every course, doc, and book comes from an actual web search
  with the current year in the query. If nothing good exists for a gap, it says so.
- **It won't pad an empty tracker.** No applications logged yet? Instead of an empty
  report, it nudges you to run [`/search`](/docs/commands/search) and
  [`/apply`](/docs/commands/apply) first, or to point it at a single job with
  `/upskill <url>`.
- **Low-priority gaps** show in the heatmap for completeness but don't get a plan entry
  unless you ask — the plan stays focused on what moves the needle.

## Seeing it in the dashboard

Every report lands in `upskill/report-*.md`, and the dashboard's **Upskill** tab lists
them for you to re-read any time. You can also kick off a fresh run straight from the
dashboard Console — it runs this same `/upskill` command.
